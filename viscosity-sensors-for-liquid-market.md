<p><p>Viscosity sensors for liquid measurement employ different methodologies, primarily classified into sonic and mechanical sensors. Sonic sensors utilize ultrasound to determine viscosity by analyzing the speed of sound through the liquid. This method is non-invasive and highly accurate. In contrast, mechanical sensors measure viscosity through direct interaction, using rotating elements or other mechanical means to assess resistance. Both types cater to various industries, helping in processes where fluid behavior is critical for quality control and efficiency.</p></p>
