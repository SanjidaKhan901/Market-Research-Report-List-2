<p><p>The drywall screws market is primarily divided into fine-thread and coarse-thread types, each designed for specific applications in construction and home improvement. Fine-thread screws are ideal for attaching drywall to metal studs, offering better grip and reduced risk of stripping. Coarse-thread screws, on the other hand, are suited for wooden studs, providing quick installation and increased holding power. The choice between these types depends on the materials being used and the overall requirements of the drywall installation process.</p></p>
