<p><p>The liquid propellant market comprises three primary types: petroleum-based fuels, cryogenic fuels, and hypergolic fuels. Petroleum propellants, derived from refined fossil fuels, are commonly used in traditional rocket engines. Cryogenic propellants, such as liquid oxygen and liquid hydrogen, are stored at extremely low temperatures and provide high performance for space missions. Hypergolic fuels ignite spontaneously upon contact with an oxidizer, offering simplicity and reliability in propulsion systems. Each type serves distinct applications and requirements in the aerospace industry.</p></p>
